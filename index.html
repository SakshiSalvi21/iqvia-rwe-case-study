Overview
End-to-end solution design for global RWE studies using a configuration-driven design surface, with strong focus on clarity, scalability, and error prevention.
Scope
~5 global studies · Multi-country deployments · Cross-device validation · Localization across 25+ languages/regions.

The Challenge

Real-World Evidence (RWE) studies at IQVIA involve collecting and interpreting data from real clinical environments to generate reliable insights for life-science clients. Designing digital solutions to support RWE studies meant juggling:

  • Diverse clinical workflows
  • Multi-country language requirements
  • Regulatory and data integrity constraints
  • Complex logic and validation rules
  • Cross-device usability
  • Rapid iteration cycles driven by stakeholder feedback

My work focused on designing study solutions that were not only functionally robust but also clear, consistent, and deployable at scale.

My Role & Contribution

As the primary designer on 5 RWE study design projects, I drove:

  • End-to-end solution design: From requirements → Sculptor configuration → release.
  • Stakeholder discovery & alignment: Collaborated with clinical researchers, operational leads, and technologists to translate needs into structured workflows.
  • Template creation & standardization: Built reusable configuration patterns to reduce variability and errors.
  • Localization & translation management: Supported solutions across 25+ languages and regions.
  • Agile iteration & feedback loops: Rapid refinement cycles based on stakeholder feedback and test outcomes.
  • Cross-device validation: Ensured workflows functioned correctly across device types before release.
  • Release coordination: Partnered with engineering and QA to deliver stable, validated solutions.

Understanding the Users

Rather than generic personas, the users I supported were:

  • Clinical researchers configuring study instruments and logic
  • Operational teams validating workflows and managing deployments
  • Global stakeholders relying on consistent behaviour across regions

These users needed:

  • Predictable behaviour from the study tools
  • Clear language and rules
  • Confidence that deployments adhered to clinical and regulatory needs

Design Approach

Systems Thinking First

Because the design surface was Sculptor — a configuration tool — I designed systems rather than screens:

  • Organized logic flows so stakeholders could reason about them naturally
  • Built configuration templates that encoded best practices
  • Ensured every element had clear meaning and predictable behavior

This wasn’t just “making it work”; it was making it understandable.

Iterative Feedback & Agile Collaboration

I built close feedback loops with stakeholders, driving decisions based on:

  • Stakeholder review sessions
  • Functional testing and edge cases
  • Cross-device validation results

Every iteration got more stable and clearer, reducing late-stage rework.

Key Design Decisions

  1. Reusable Configuration Templates
    I abstracted repeatable patterns (e.g., consent flows, symptom logic, scheduling rules) into curated templates that could be reused and adapted. This improved consistency and reduced configuration time by ~30%.
  2. Language-Aware Structuring
    Because studies ran across 25+ languages, I structured content and logic for translation readiness. That meant keeping copy modular and validating translations in context, preventing mismatches between logic and text.
  3. Validation & Constraint Rules
    I introduced explicit system constraints in Sculptor to prevent invalid combinations of logic and content. These safeguards reduced runtime errors and improved data integrity across deployments.

Testing & Quality Assurance

Designing systems at scale meant testing is design. I led:

  • Functional validation across device types
  • Scenario testing for edge-case behaviors
  • UAT cycles with clinical and operational stakeholders

Errors caught early in these design-oriented tests saved time and reduced downstream fixes by ~25%.

Results & Impact

  • Designed and deployed solutions for multiple RWE studies across global regions.
  • Standardized templates that improved consistency and scalability of future studies.
  • Reduced configuration errors and rework through clear logic and validation rules.
  • Ensured language integrity across 25+ translations.
  • Strengthened collaboration between clinical, operational, and technical teams.
  • Ultimately, these solutions delivered reliable, globally consistent study tools that supported real research outcomes.

Reflection

This work reinforced that design isn’t only about pixels. In complex systems with high stakes, good design is about:

  • Clarity under complexity
  • Patterns that scale
  • Language that communicates intent
  • Structures that prevent error

I enjoy designing products that empower other builders — and this project sharpened that skill in a high-stakes, global context.
