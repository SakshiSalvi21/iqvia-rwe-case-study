The Challenge

RWE studies require designing digital solutions that work across diverse clinical workflows, regions, and devices, while maintaining data integrity and audit readiness. The design surface was Sculptor, a configuration environment where logic, content, validation rules, and localization must fit together cleanly.

My Role & Ownership

  • Led requirements discovery and translated stakeholder needs into structured workflows and system logic.
  • Designed reusable templates and standard patterns to reduce variability and error.
  • Managed localization and translation across 25+ countries and validated language in context.
  • Ran cross-device testing, UAT workflows, and supported release/deployment.
  • Iterated in an agile feedback loop with clinical, operational, QA, and engineering teams.

Understanding the Users

Users needed predictable system behavior, clear language, and confidence that configurations adhered to clinical and regulatory requirements.

Design Approach

Systems thinking first

  • Structured logic flows so stakeholders could reason about behavior and edge cases.
  • Encoded best practices into templates to reduce cognitive load and speed delivery.
  • Made meaning explicit through naming, rules, and constraints to prevent invalid states.

Iterative feedback & agile collaboration

I built tight feedback loops with stakeholders, refining configurations based on review sessions, test outcomes, and implementation constraints.

Key Design Decisions

  1. Reusable configuration templates: standardized repeatable patterns to improve consistency and speed.
  2. Language-aware structuring: modularized content and validated translations in context to avoid logic-text mismatches.
  3. Validation & constraint rules: introduced safeguards to reduce runtime errors and protect data integrity.

Testing, Release & Quality

  • Cross-device functional validation and scenario testing for edge cases.
  • UAT cycles with clinical and operational stakeholders.
  • Release readiness checks and post-deployment verification.

Results & Impact

  • Supported global deployments across multiple regions with consistent behavior.
  • Reduced configuration errors and rework through clearer patterns and validation.
  • Maintained language integrity across 25+ translations.
  • Strengthened cross-functional alignment through clearer logic and documentation.

Reflection

This work reinforced that design isn’t only pixels. In complex systems, good design is clarity under constraints: patterns that scale, language that communicates intent, and structures that prevent error.
